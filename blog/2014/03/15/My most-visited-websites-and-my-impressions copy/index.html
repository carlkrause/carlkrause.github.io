My Most-visited Websites and My Impressions

Mar 15th, 2014

This week I’m taking a look at three websites I use regularly and jotting down some thoughts about my experience navigating them. All three provide a superb product and their sites are reflective of their unique brands and audiences.

Soundcloud

I love the service Soundcloud provides; they’ve revolutionized streaming audio and created a massive community of podcast and music-lovers. The page’s central focus is an image of someone wearing headphones or near a device (demonstrating the functionality clearly), and includes, in bold text, a direct call to action: “Hear the world’s sounds.” It provides a gateway to the trove of audio in a simple way that is: bright, transparent, inspiring, clear, and, well, pretty. It’s very clear how one can sign up, search, and explore. Browsing is very easy and the three-color scheme is handsome. It doesn’t sell anything up front other than joining the community. The site more than does its job: I ended up browsing instead of writing this.

The Art Institute of Chicago

I have a strong affinity for the Art Institute; it’s a vitally important art museum. My eye is drawn to the changing exhibition images at the center, which show what is on at the museum. Overall, though, there’s no central focus and lots of clutter. I’d describe the site as colorful, cluttered, inconsistent, content-rich, and confusing. It is hard to sift through the many available options (many of which seem similar), and it is hard to browse the site. I feel overwhelmed and distracted after spending too much time with it. It sells the experience of visiting, but most likely those visiting the site are already sold on it.

Vfiles

I love Vfiles—it’s the commerical and social media hub of the growing New York—based streetwear resurgence. They have great visual and video content and provides an enjoyable way of staying on top of streetwear trends. On the site, I’m drawn to the central logo and scrolling Ms. Piggy images—-it very succinctly communicates the weird-is-good identity of the brand. It’s elegant, fun, silly, simple, and organized. It’s also quite easy to find out which direction to head from the toolbar. I’ve made purchases before so the site does a good job of making the experience as enjoyable as the products it sells.
